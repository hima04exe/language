International Mother Language Day or Martyrs' Day

International Mother Language Day or Martyrs' Day is observed on February 21st every year, is a global celebration of linguistic and cultural diversity. Established by UNESCO in 1999, this day honors the right of people to speak, learn, and preserve their native languages. The significance of this day traces back to the Language Movement in Bangladesh, where students sacrificed their lives in 1952 to protect their mother tongue, Bengali. Their struggle became a symbol of linguistic rights worldwide, emphasizing the need to safeguard endangered languages and promote multilingual education. International Mother Language Day serves as a reminder that languages are more than just communication tools—they carry history, identity, and heritage, shaping the cultural fabric of societies.
